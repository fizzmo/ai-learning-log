The Nine Units

The course framework at a glance, with approximate AP exam weighting for each unit.

1

The Global Tapestry

1200–1450 · 8–10%

State-building in Song China, Dar al-Islam, South & Southeast Asia, the Americas, Africa, and Europe.

2

Networks of Exchange

1200–1450 · 8–10%

The Silk Roads, the Mongol Empire, the Indian Ocean, trans-Saharan routes, and cultural diffusion.

3

Land-Based Empires

1450–1750 · 12–15%

The Gunpowder Empires — Ottoman, Safavid, Mughal, Qing — and how they expanded, administered, and legitimized power.

4

Transoceanic Interconnections

1450–1750 · 12–15%

European exploration, the Columbian Exchange, maritime empires, the Atlantic slave trade, and global silver flows.

5

Revolutions

1750–1900 · 12–15%

The Enlightenment and the Atlantic revolutions — American, French, Haitian, Latin American — plus industrialization's start.

6

Consequences of Industrialization

1750–1900 · 12–15%

Imperialism's new wave, economic imperialism in Asia, migration, and resistance from the Sepoy Rebellion to the Zulu.

7

Global Conflict

1900–present · 8–10%

Two world wars, the Depression between them, mass atrocities, and the collapse of the old imperial order.

8

Cold War & Decolonization

1900–present · 8–10%

Superpower rivalry, proxy wars, newly independent states, and non-aligned movements across Asia and Africa.

9

Globalization

1900–present · 8–10%

Technology, economics, environment, and culture in an interconnected world — and the debates over its winners and losers.

Six Course Themes

The threads to trace through every unit — and the lenses the exam expects you to use.

🌍

Humans & the Environment (ENV)

Disease, climate, migration, and how people reshape — and are reshaped by — their surroundings.

🎭

Cultural Developments (CDI)

Religions, ideologies, arts, and sciences: how ideas spread, blend, and transform societies.

🏛️

Governance (GOV)

How states form, expand, legitimize power, and decline — from khanates to nation-states.

⚖️

Economic Systems (ECN)

Trade networks, labor systems, capitalism, and the long road to a global economy.

👥

Social Organization (SIO)

Hierarchies of class, gender, race, and caste — how societies order themselves and who pushes back.

⚙️

Technology & Innovation (TEC)

From the compass and gunpowder to the steam engine and the internet — tools that change everything.
