Newton's Principia Mathematica

Peter the Great Founds St. Petersburg

A "window on the West" built on a swamp: Russia westernizes by decree — beards taxed, nobles drilled, an empire turned toward Europe.

The Enlightenment between two covers: 28 volumes to "change the general way of thinking" — reason, tolerance, and progress against throne and altar.

The Seven Years' War

The first truly global war — fought in Europe, America, and India. Britain wins an empire; France's debts plant the seeds of revolution.

The French Revolution Begins

Napoleon Crowns Himself Emperor

The revolution's general becomes its monarch. His Code and conquests export revolutionary ideas — and provoke the nationalism that undoes him.

Bismarck's "blood and iron" — three quick wars — forge the German Empire, proclaimed in France's own palace. Europe's balance of power tilts permanently.

The Berlin Conference

The New Imperialism at full speed: European powers partition Africa for markets, materials, and prestige — with consequences that still echo.

The Nine Units

The course framework at a glance — each unit carries roughly 10–15% of the AP exam.

1

Renaissance & Exploration

1450–1648 · 10–15%

Italian and Northern humanism, Renaissance art, the printing press, and Europe's first overseas empires.

2

Age of Reformation

1450–1648 · 10–15%

Luther, Calvin, and Henry VIII; the Catholic response; and the wars of religion through Westphalia.

3

Absolutism & Constitutionalism

1648–1815 · 10–15%

Louis XIV versus the English model; Peter the Great; mercantilism, the Dutch Golden Age, and balance-of-power politics.

4

Scientific & Philosophical Developments

1648–1815 · 10–15%

The Scientific Revolution and the Enlightenment — Newton, Locke, Voltaire, Rousseau, and enlightened despots.

5

Conflict, Crisis & Reaction

1648–1815 · 10–15%

The Seven Years' War, the French Revolution, Napoleon's empire, and the conservative settlement at Vienna.

6

Industrialization & Its Effects

1815–1914 · 10–15%

Steam and steel, urbanization and class, reform movements, and the New Imperialism's scramble for the globe.

7

19th-Century Perspectives

1815–1914 · 10–15%

The age of -isms: liberalism, nationalism, socialism, romanticism, realism — and the unifications of Italy and Germany.

8

20th-Century Global Conflicts

1914–present · 10–15%

Two world wars, the Russian Revolution, fascism and the Depression, and the Holocaust.

9

Cold War & Contemporary Europe

1914–present · 10–15%

A divided continent, decolonization, 1989's peaceful revolutions, and the European Union's promise and strains.

Seven Course Themes

The lenses the exam expects you to apply across all four periods.

🌐

Interaction of Europe & the World (INT)

Exploration, colonization, imperialism, and decolonization — Europe abroad, and the world's effect on Europe.

💰

Economic & Commercial Developments (ECD)

From mercantilism to industrial capitalism to the welfare state and the single market.

📜

Cultural & Intellectual Developments (CID)

Humanism, the Enlightenment, romanticism, modernism — how Europeans understood themselves and their world.

👑

States & Other Institutions of Power (SOP)

Absolutism, constitutionalism, total war, totalitarianism, and supranational union.

🏘️

Social Organization & Development (SCD)

Family, class, gender, and the transformations wrought by plague, industry, and war.

🚩

National & European Identity (NEI)

Nationalism's rise, its catastrophes, and the contested idea of a shared European identity.

🔬

Technological & Scientific Innovation (TSI)

The printing press, the Scientific Revolution, industrial technology, and the digital age.
