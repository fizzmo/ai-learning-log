I put together two interactive history study timelines and published them here so
they're easy to share. Both were generated with the **Fable AI model from
Anthropic** — a nice example of what these models can do: take a dense subject and
turn it into a clean, navigable, self-contained web page in one pass.

Each page is a single HTML file — no logins, no setup. Just open and explore the
units, themes, and key turning points:

- 📜 **[AP European History — 1450 to the Present]({{ '/timelines/ap-european-history.html' | relative_url }})** — from the Renaissance to a united Europe.
- 🌍 **[AP World History: Modern — 1200 CE to the Present]({{ '/timelines/ap-world-history.html' | relative_url }})** — eight centuries of a connected world.

What I like about these as an AI artifact: the model handled the history *and* the
design — structuring nine units, weaving in the course themes, and styling it all
into something genuinely pleasant to read. A handy study aid, and an easy link to
pass around the family.
